Earnings vs Debt

An analysis

By TK TK

January 22, 2019

New government data reveal the student debt levels and first-year earnings of Columbia graduates based on what they studied, broken out by major or graduate degree program. Until now, the government has only published schoolwide statistics on debt and earnings for undergraduates.

In the last decade, the number of students majoring in the social sciences has skyrocketed while that in the humanities has continued to fall. Computer science and certain engineering fields have seen a large growth in popularity.

Arts

In creative arts fields like film and writing, master’s students at Columbia accumulate much more debt than master’s students at peer programs, with little to no gain in income.

The first three of the four fields shown above leave students with the highest debt compared to other programs at Columbia while leaving them with the lowest incomes.

Bachelor’s students in the arts make less income, but take on significantly less debt.

Students majoring in Visual and Performing Arts or Rhetoric and Composition/Writing Studies accumulate more debt than first-year earnings on average; these are the only such majors at Columbia.
